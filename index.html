The Soviet impact on newborns

The Soviet union occupied the Baltic nations (Estonia, Latvia, lithuania) both before the second world war(1939,1940), and continuing after german troops retreated (1944-1991). The impact on the wellbeing of these nations is quite significant. Ever since 1991 when the Soviet union ceased occupation, child mortality has dropped significantly. However, disproportionately compared to surrounding nations such as Finland.
